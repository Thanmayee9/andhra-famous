Coringa-Wildlife-Sanctuary

Extensive Mangrove Ecosystem:

Coringa Wildlife Sanctuary is one of India's largest mangrove forests, covering about 235 square kilometers near Kakinada, and accounts for roughly 40% of Andhra Pradeshâ€™s mangrove cover.

Rich Biodiversity:

Sanctuary is home to over 120 bird species, including critically endangered white-backed and long-billed vultures, as well as rare mammals like the fishing cat and smooth-coated otter. It also serves as a breeding ground for Olive Ridley turtles and supports diverse flora and fauna adapted to estuarine and tidal environments.

Vital Ecological Role:

The mangroves of Coringa protect coastal areas from erosion and storm surges, help maintain ecological balance, and provide important breeding and nursery grounds for fish and other aquatic species.



Someshwara Swamy Temple

Ancient and Sacred Shiva Temple:

The Bhimavaram Someshwara Swamy Temple, also known as Somarama, is one of the five Pancharama Kshetras revered in Andhra Pradesh and is dedicated to Lord Shiva. The temple dates back to the 3rdâ€“10th centuries AD and features a prominent gopuram (entrance tower), a lotus-filled pond called Chandrakundam, and a unique Shiva linga that changes color with the lunar phasesâ€”white on full moon nights and black on new moon nights.

Rich Legends and Festivals:

According to legend, the Shiva linga here was installed by the Moon God (Chandra) after the demon Tarakasura was defeated and the Atma Linga was split into five parts. Major festivals celebrated at the temple include Maha Shivaratri and Sarannavarathri, drawing large numbers of devotees for special rituals and processions.

Architectural and Cultural Significance:

The temple complex is expansive, featuring multiple shrines (including those for Annapurna, Hanuman, and Rama), a large Nandi statue, and a 15-meter pillar in front of the gopuram. It serves as a center for spiritual gatherings, weddings, and community events, and is recognized for its historical inscriptions and unique architectural features.



Dindi Resorts/Palavelli Resorts

Scenic Riverfront Setting and Village Experience:

Both Dindi and Palavelli Resorts are located on the banks of the Godavari River in the lush Konaseema region, offering picturesque views, coconut groves, and a tranquil village atmosphere that immerses guests in the local culture and landscape.

Unique Accommodation and Activities:

Palavelli Resorts recreates traditional Konaseema village life with options like Pond Villas, Earth Villas, and Manduva houses, while Dindi resorts offer modern amenities, houseboat stays, boating, island hopping, and village canal cruises for a memorable and immersive experience.

Ideal for Relaxation and Gatherings:

These resorts provide multi-cuisine restaurants, banquet and conference facilities, and are popular for family vacations, friendsâ€™ get-togethers, and corporate outings, making them top choices for leisure and rejuvenation in West Godavari.



Shivalayam

Unique Hanuman-Installed Shiva Lingam:

Sivadevuni Chikkala Shivalayam is renowned for its large white Shiva lingam, believed to have been brought from Kashi and installed by Lord Hanuman during his journey for Lord Rama, as per the Ramayana legend.

Ancient Temple with Fertility Belief:

The temple, constructed by the King of Mogalturu several centuries ago, is over 1,000 years old and is especially famous for the belief that couples desiring children can plant a coconut tree in the temple garden to have their wish fulfilled.

Architectural and Natural Features:

The temple complex includes shrines for Goddess Parvati and Lakshmi Narayana, and is set beside a large, scenic lake, adding to its spiritual and cultural significance in West Godavari.
