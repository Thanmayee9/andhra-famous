Godavari Specials...

Pootharekulu

Delicate Rice Paper Sweet:

Athreyapuram Pootharekulu is a traditional sweet from Atreyapuram, Andhra Pradesh, made by wrapping powdered sugar, ghee, and sometimes dry fruits in ultra-thin, translucent rice starch sheets, giving it the nickname "paper sweet".

Cultural and Economic Importance:

This sweet is a hallmark of Telugu festivals and weddings, and its production has supported a cottage industry in Atreyapuram for over three centuries, providing livelihoods to hundreds of local families.

Unique Preparation Method:

The rice sheets are crafted by spreading rice batter on a hot, inverted earthen pot, then carefully layering and folding with fillings, showcasing the skill and tradition of local artisans.



Chepala pulusu

Tangy Tamarind-Based Gravy:

West Godavari Chepala Pulusu is a signature Andhra fish curry known for its tangy and spicy gravy made with tamarind juice, onions, green chilies, and a blend of traditional spices, giving it a distinctive flavor.

Slow-Cooked for Rich Flavor:

The fish is gently simmered in the masala and tamarind gravy, allowing the flavors to infuse deeply; the curry is often said to taste even better the next day as the fish and spices meld together.

Served with Rice:

Dish is typically enjoyed with hot steamed rice, and is a staple in festive and everyday meals in the Godavari region.



Gongura pachadi

Signature Tangy Chutney:

West Godavari Gongura Pachadi is a traditional Andhra chutney made from fresh gongura (red sorrel) leaves, known for its distinctive tangy and slightly sour flavor, often enhanced with red chilies, garlic, and a tempering of mustard and cumin seeds.

Staple with Rice:

This pachadi is commonly served with hot steamed rice and a dollop of ghee, making it a staple accompaniment in everyday meals and festive occasions across the Godavari region.

Homemade and Preserved:

The chutney is typically prepared at home, sometimes in large batches, and can be stored for weeks, allowing families to enjoy its unique taste over time.



Muntha Masala

Popular Spicy Puffed Rice Snack:

West Godavari Muntha Masala is a beloved Andhra street food made primarily with puffed rice, mixed with ingredients like onions, tomatoes, peanuts, cornflakes, sev, and a blend of spices and lemon juice for a tangy, spicy flavor.
